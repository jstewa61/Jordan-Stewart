Alzheimers Awareness Interactive Campaign

Alzheimer's Disease is a growing public health concern as it becomes more common among the aging population. According to the Alzheimer's Association, 5.8 Million Americans alone suffer from the disease and is projected to rise to 14 Million by 2050. It is the 6th leading cause of death in the U.S. and kills more than Breast Cancer and Prostate Cancer combined.

The goal of this project was to explore using a narrative of videos to convert donations. The results were a series of video ads that direct users toward a landing website to accept donations to fund research.

Website

While this was a great exercise in sharpening development skills, an extensive amount of research would have been done to create a working website. Nonetheless, it is a great example of a high fidelity, interactive wireframe. The website's main focus is on providing facts and accepting donations.

View the website prototype here

Video Campaign

To get users to go to the website, they are presented with videos that are meant to be provacative and bring urgency to the cause. Each video features thoughtful typography and photos of intimate moments that could be lost, but hopefully research can provide clues to a cure to save.
